## AB-2826: California Platform Accountability and Transparency Act.

>[!tldr] **AB2826 TL;DR Summary**
> Assembly Bill 2826, the California Platform Accountability and Transparency Act (C-PATA), would require large online platforms (with at least 25 million U.S. monthly users) to provide qualified data to approved university-affiliated researchers for the purpose of studying platform activity, under strict privacy and cybersecurity safeguards set by the Department of Technology. The bill mandates platforms to make ongoing disclosures about widely shared or policy-violating content, advertising, and algorithmic practices, while protecting user privacy and business confidentiality. It establishes annual reporting requirements, civil penalties for noncompliance, and legal protections for researchers and journalists conducting public-interest investigations. The Department is tasked with issuing regulations, ensuring transparency, and balancing public access with privacy and trade secret protections.
