## AB-412: Generative artificial intelligence: training data: copyrighted materials.

>[!tldr] **AB412 TL;DR Summary**
> California Assembly Bill 412, introduced by Assembly Member Bauer-Kahan in 2025, requires developers of generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) models to document and disclose any known copyrighted materials used in training their models. Developers must provide a mechanism on their website for copyright owners to request information by submitting registration numbers and content fingerprints, and must respond within seven days with details about any of the owner's copyrighted materials likely present in the training data. Failure to comply can result in civil action and financial penalties. The bill exempts developers who make all training data publicly available at no cost and defines key terms such as 'developer,' 'GenAI,' and 'copyrighted material.'
