## SB-238: Workplace surveillance tools.

>[!tldr] **SB238 TL;DR Summary**
> Senate Bill 238, introduced by Senator Smallwood-Cuevas in the California Legislature's 2025–2026 session, requires employers—including public entities—to annually disclose to the Department of Industrial Relations all workplace surveillance tools they use. The notice must detail the surveillance technology, data collected from workers and consumers, opt-out options for personal data collection, and third parties with data access. The department must make these disclosures publicly available online within 30 days, enhancing transparency around workplace monitoring and data practices.
