## AB-1008: California Consumer Privacy Act of 2018: personal information.

>[!tldr] **AB1008 TL;DR Summary**
> Assembly Bill No. 1008 amends Section 1798.140 of the California Civil Code, clarifying and updating definitions under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA). The bill specifies that personal information can exist in various formats, including physical, digital, and abstract digital forms such as those produced by artificial intelligence systems. It maintains and strengthens consumer privacy rights, outlines business obligations regarding the collection, use, and sharing of personal information, and ensures alignment with the intent of the CPRA. The bill also incorporates changes contingent on the enactment of SB 1223 and affirms the Legislature's commitment to advancing privacy protections.
