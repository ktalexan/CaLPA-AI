## SB-420: Automated decision systems.

>[!tldr] **SB420 TL;DR Summary**
> Senate Bill 420, introduced by Senator Padilla in California's 2025–2026 legislative session, aims to regulate the use of high-risk automated decision systems (AI) that significantly impact individuals in areas like employment, housing, education, and healthcare. The bill requires developers and deployers of such systems to conduct impact assessments to identify and mitigate risks of algorithmic discrimination, maintain governance programs, and provide transparency and human review options for affected individuals. State agencies must keep these assessments confidential, and the Attorney General or Civil Rights Department is authorized to enforce compliance with penalties for violations. Entities with 50 or fewer employees and systems approved by certain federal agencies are exempt, and the bill prohibits state contracts with violators of these or related civil rights laws.
