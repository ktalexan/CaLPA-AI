## AB-392: Pornographic internet websites: consent.

>[!tldr] **AB392 TL;DR Summary**
> California Assembly Bill 392, introduced by Assembly Member Dixon, would require operators of pornographic internet websites to ensure that all sexually explicit content uploaded to their platforms does not include individuals who did not consent, were minors at the time of creation, or did not consent to the upload. Users must submit a statement under penalty of perjury confirming the age and consent of depicted individuals before uploading content. If a depicted individual notifies the operator of non-consent or being a minor, the operator must remove the content within 48 hours. The bill allows depicted individuals and public prosecutors to pursue civil actions against violators, with significant damages and penalties, and specifically includes imagery generated by artificial intelligence.
