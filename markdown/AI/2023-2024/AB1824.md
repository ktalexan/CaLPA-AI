## AB-1824: California Consumer Privacy Act of 2018: opt out right: mergers.

>[!tldr] **AB1824 TL;DR Summary**
> Assembly Bill No. 1824 amends the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) to require that when a business transfers a consumer's personal information as part of a merger, acquisition, bankruptcy, or similar transaction, the receiving business must honor any prior opt-out requests made by the consumer to the original business. The bill reinforces consumer rights to opt out of the sale or sharing of their personal information, extends protections to minors, and clarifies compliance obligations for businesses in such transactions. It is designed to further the purposes of the California Privacy Rights Act of 2020 and contains provisions that coordinate with other pending legislation.
