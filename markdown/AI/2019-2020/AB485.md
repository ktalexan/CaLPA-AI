## AB-485: Local government: economic development subsidies.

>[!tldr] **AB485 TL;DR Summary**
> Assembly Bill No. 485 (Chapter 803), effective January 1, 2020, requires California local agencies to publicly disclose detailed information and hold hearings before approving economic development subsidies of $100,000 or more for warehouse distribution centers. The bill mandates annual public reports and hearings on the subsidies, covering job creation, wage scales, use of temporary agencies, benefit packages, disadvantaged worker hiring, automation or artificial intelligence impacts, and accountability measures. Local agencies must submit these reports to the Governor’s Office of Business and Economic Development for public posting, and are prohibited from signing nondisclosure agreements regarding such subsidies. These transparency requirements apply to all cities, including charter cities, to ensure public access to government information.
