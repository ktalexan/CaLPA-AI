## AB-2058: Devices:disclosures.

>[!tldr] **AB2058 TL;DR Summary**
> Assembly Bill No. 2058, introduced by Assembly Member Weber in the 2023–2024 California legislative session, requires that, starting January 1, 2027, any device that collects or analyzes physiological data (such as heartbeat, blood pressure, or blood oxygen) and is not regulated under specified federal laws must include a clear disclosure about known limitations of its effectiveness for certain populations based on characteristics like age, color, disability, ethnicity, gender, or race. The bill expands existing public health and safety regulations, creates a new crime for noncompliance, and does not require state reimbursement to local agencies or school districts for costs incurred due to the new requirements.
