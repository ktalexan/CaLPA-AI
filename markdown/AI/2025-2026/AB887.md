## AB-887: Pupil instruction: high schools: computer science courses: implementation guide.

>[!tldr] **AB887 TL;DR Summary**
> Assembly Bill 887, introduced in California's 2025–2026 legislative session, would require all public high schools, including charter schools, to offer at least one computer science course by the 2029–30 school year, with phased implementation starting in 2027. The bill mandates that school districts and charter schools develop and publicly post plans to expand computer science access, with specific strategies to increase enrollment among underrepresented groups such as female students, students with disabilities, students from ethnic and racial minorities, and those eligible for free or reduced-price meals. Annual progress reviews and public reporting are required, along with statewide data collection on course offerings and enrollment demographics. Additionally, the State Department of Education must create a comprehensive implementation guide for computer science courses, and if the bill imposes state-mandated costs, reimbursement procedures are outlined.
