## AB-887: Pupil instruction: high schools: computer science courses: implementation guide.

>[!tldr] **AB887 TL;DR Summary**
> Assembly Bill 887, introduced in the California Legislature for the 2025–2026 session, would require all public high schools and charter schools serving grades 9–12 to offer at least one computer science course by the 2029–30 school year, with phased implementation beginning in 2027. School districts and charter schools must adopt, publicly post, and annually review plans to expand computer science course offerings, specifically outlining efforts to increase participation among underrepresented groups such as female students, students with disabilities, and those from ethnic minorities or low-income backgrounds. The bill also mandates annual public reporting of disaggregated enrollment data and directs the State Department of Education to develop a comprehensive computer science implementation guide by July 2026, including resources and best practices. If the state determines that the bill imposes additional costs on local agencies, reimbursement procedures are provided.
