## SB-1154: California Preventing Algorithmic Collusion Act of 2024.

>[!tldr] **SB1154 TL;DR Summary**
> Senate Bill 1154, the California Preventing Algorithmic Collusion Act of 2024, aims to regulate the use of pricing algorithms in California by prohibiting the use or distribution of algorithms trained with nonpublic competitor data, requiring companies with $5 million or more in annual revenue to disclose to customers and workers when prices or commercial terms are set by algorithms, and mandating detailed reporting to the Attorney General upon request. The law authorizes civil penalties for violations, establishes presumptions of antitrust violations under certain circumstances, and requires a public study on the prevalence and effects of pricing algorithms by 2027. Reports submitted under the Act are treated as confidential trade secrets, and the Attorney General may seek technical assistance from the National Institute of Standards and Technology.
