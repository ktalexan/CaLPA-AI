## SB-711: Taxation: federal conformity.

>[!tldr] **SB711 TL;DR Summary**
> Senate Bill 711, as amended in March 2025, updates California's Revenue and Taxation Code to conform state tax laws to the federal Internal Revenue Code as of January 1, 2025, for taxable years beginning on or after that date. The bill makes numerous substantive changes to both the Personal Income Tax Law and the Corporation Tax Law by adopting federal tax provisions enacted after January 1, 2015, except where specifically excluded or modified. These changes affect areas such as tax credits, deductions, net operating losses, Roth IRAs, capital assets, and more, while also repealing obsolete provisions and making technical and clarifying amendments. The bill requires the Franchise Tax Board to analyze and publicly report on these changes, and it takes effect immediately as an urgency statute. Notably, the bill was amended to remove previous provisions regarding school nutrition and guardian meal reimbursement, focusing solely on taxation and federal conformity.
