## AB-1170: Maintenance of the codes.

>[!tldr] **AB1170 TL;DR Summary**
> California Assembly Bill 1170, introduced for the 2025–2026 session, is an omnibus 'maintenance of the codes' bill that implements a wide range of nonsubstantive, technical, and clarifying amendments across numerous sections of California state law, spanning the Business and Professions, Civil, Corporations, Education, Elections, Fish and Game, Food and Agricultural, Government, Health and Safety, Insurance, Labor, Military and Veterans, Penal, Public Contract, Public Resources, Public Utilities, Revenue and Taxation, Streets and Highways, Unemployment Insurance, Vehicle, Water, and Welfare and Institutions Codes, among others. The bill incorporates recommendations from the Legislative Counsel to update statutory language, correct cross-references, modernize terminology (including changes related to artificial intelligence, digital privacy, and technology), and adjust formatting and grammar, without making substantive policy changes. It also repeals obsolete code headings, renumbers certain sections, and ensures consistency with federal law and recent legislative enactments. AB 1170 does not appropriate funds, require a fiscal committee, or create new local programs, and is intended to keep California's statutory codes current, accurate, and internally consistent.
