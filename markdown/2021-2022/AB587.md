## AB-587: Social media companies: terms of service.

>[!tldr] **AB587 TL;DR Summary**
> California Assembly Bill No. 587 requires large social media companies to publicly post clear and accessible terms of service for each platform they operate, detailing permitted user behaviors and potential actions against violations. Starting in 2024, these companies must submit semiannual reports to the Attorney General outlining their content moderation policies, definitions and handling of categories like hate speech, misinformation, and extremism, as well as data on flagged and actioned content, including the use of automated systems and appeals. The Attorney General will make these reports publicly available online. Companies that fail to comply face significant civil penalties, with enforcement actions possible by the Attorney General or certain city attorneys. The law exempts smaller companies and platforms with limited user interactions.
