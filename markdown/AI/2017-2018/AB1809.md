## AB-1809: Higher education trailer bill.

>[!tldr] **AB1809 TL;DR Summary**
> Assembly Bill No. 1809 (Chapter 33), enacted on June 27, 2018, is a comprehensive higher education budget trailer bill for California that implements major reforms and funding allocations for the state's public postsecondary education system, especially the California Community Colleges. Key provisions include establishing a new Student Success Funding Formula that rewards colleges for serving low-income students and improving student outcomes, creating the California Online Community College to expand accessible and flexible online education for working adults, and introducing new grant programs for student completion, STEM pathways, apprenticeship innovation, reentry support for formerly incarcerated individuals, and open educational resources. The bill also increases oversight and reporting requirements, revises Cal Grant and Middle Class Scholarship awards, improves support for foster youth, refugees, and veterans, expands mental health and basic needs services, and appropriates hundreds of millions of dollars for technology, hunger relief, and other programs. It mandates new accountability and equity measures, streamlines financial aid, and encourages adoption of adaptive learning and artificial intelligence technologies to close achievement gaps and improve educational outcomes.
