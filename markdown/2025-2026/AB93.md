## AB-93: Water resources: demands: data centers.

>[!tldr] **AB93 TL;DR Summary**
> Assembly Bill 93, introduced by Assembly Member Papan, would require owners or operators of data centers in California to provide estimates and annual reports of water usage when applying for or renewing business licenses with cities or counties, under penalty of perjury. The bill mandates that local jurisdictions set water efficiency standards for data centers, which may vary based on the size and type of the facility. It also directs the Department of Water Resources and the State Energy Resources Conservation and Development Commission to develop guidelines for maximizing resource efficiency in technology sectors, consistent with urban water use goals. Public entities must include data center water usage in their water demand analyses. The bill applies statewide, including to charter cities, and outlines conditions for state reimbursement of local costs.
